Funding Opportunities for Your Research

We are excited to host you in our lab for your PhD or Postdoc journey.

Depending on your nationality and university, there are many opportunities to partly or fully fund your PhD or postdoc journey. NUS has a great list of scholarship opportunities. Please see below for selected examples of funding opportunities. We are eligible to host awardees of these fellowships.

General (Open to Many or All Nationalities)
  • Lee Kuan Yew Postdoctoral Fellowship: A prestigious postdoctoral funding option for up to 3 years, open to nationals of all countries, to promote the development of young and outstanding academics in science, medicine, and engineering.
  • The Singapore International Graduate Award – SINGA: Offered by A*STAR, this award can be used to fund a PhD study at our lab. You may also want to check the A*STAR Graduate Scholarship.
  • The Branco Weiss Fellowship: Awards young researchers around the world with a generous personal research grant, giving them the freedom to work on any topic they choose for up to five years.
  • NUS Commonwealth Scholarship: Citizens of Commonwealth countries (there are 53 countries in the Commonwealth of Nations, including India, Australia, Canada, New Zealand, Malaysia, several in Africa and the Caribbean, and a few European ones like Cyprus, Malta, and the UK) can consider applying for the NUS Commonwealth Scholarship, which includes a monthly stipend and tuition fees.
  • The Schlumberger Foundation Faculty for the Future: This program provides fellowship awards for women in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) from developing and emerging countries to pursue a PhD or a postdoc abroad.
  • AXA Fellowships: For researchers who completed a PhD degree up to 5 years before the application.
  • The SG Academies ASEAN Fellowship Programme: Aims to attract bright postgraduate researchers from ASEAN countries to Singapore research institutions for a 2-year long fellowship.

Country-specific

  • The Netherlands: The Rubicon program of the Dutch Research Council (NWO) offers a fellowship for postdocs abroad for recent PhD graduates from a university in the Netherlands. You may also be interested in the Niels Stensen Fellowship.
  • Switzerland: The Swiss National Science Foundation has a similar fellowship.
  • Indonesia: For its citizens, LPDP (Layanan Beasiswa dan Pendanan Riset Indonesia) offers scholarships for funding a PhD study abroad.
  • Spain: Spanish citizens may benefit from the La Fundación Ramón Areces, which offers scholarships to carry out research work in universities or research centers abroad.
  • The Philippines: Filipino citizens younger than 45 years and currently faculty members of a university that is part of the Engineering Research and Development for Technology consortium may be eligible for the ERDT PhD Foreign Scholarship.
  • Canada: Canadians can apply for a scholarship to the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC), which funds overseas doctoral programs. The Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council (SSHRC) may be another option to consider depending on the subject matter.
  • China (PRC): PRC nationals may want to consider the China Scholarship Council (CSC; 国家留学基金管理委员会) grants, available for both studying abroad and for research visits.
  • Hong Kong SAR: Permanent residents may want to check the Croucher Fellowships for Postdoctoral Research.
  • India: The ASEAN-India Science, Technology & Innovation Cooperation may be of interest to scholars from India.

Note that some of this information was compiled by Filip Biljecki, who also cites ECRcentral’s Funding schemes and fellowships for early career researchers.
