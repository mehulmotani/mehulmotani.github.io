Mehul Motani (S'92-M'01-SM'15-F'18) received the B.E. degree from Cooper Union, the M.S. degree from Syracuse University, and the Ph.D. degree from Cornell University. He is currently a faculty member in the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at the National University of Singapore and a Visiting Research Collaborator at Princeton University. Previously, he was a Visiting Fellow at Princeton University, a Research Scientist at the Institute for Infocomm Research in Singapore and a Systems Engineer at Lockheed Martin in Syracuse, NY. His research interests include information and coding theory, AI & machine learning, biomedical informatics, wireless and sensor networks, and the Internet-of-Things. Dr. Motani was the recipient of the Intel Foundation Fellowship for his Ph.D. research, the NUS Annual Teaching Excellence Award, the NUS College of Design and Engineering Teaching Excellence Award. the NUS Faculty of Engineering Innovative Teaching Award, and the NUS Faculty of Engineering Teaching Honours List Award. He is a Fellow of the IEEE and the AAIA. He has served as the Secretary of the IEEE Information Theory Society Board of Governors, as a member of IEEE Fellows Evaluation Committee, and as an Associate Editor for both the IEEE Transactions on Information Theory and the IEEE Transactions on Communications.
